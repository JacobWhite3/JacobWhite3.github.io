About me

My Name is Jacob White. I am an undergraduate student at The University of New Mexico studying Envrionmental Planning and Design with a minor in Sustainability Studies. My anticipated graduation date is Fall of 2025. My undergraduate coursework and my career goals have been guided by my strong passion to make positive commmunity change in my hometown of ALbuquerque, New Mexico. I am currently an Ecological Restoration practioner working for a non-profit called Rio Grande Return. I've been working in the field of restoration for two years and also have prior experience landscaping. The intersections of My passion for the outdoors, trying to foster a more sustainable landscape that nurtures the human spirit and more than human world, passion for collective community transformation, and environmental justice have all influenced my desire to practice ecological restoration and approach it from an interdisciplinary approach emphasizing on the socio-ecological component of this work. I enjoy running, volunteering with other community organizations and partners, reading, and exploring the great outdoors! I am outside every opportunity I get and I deeply enjoy any opportunity I get to work with plants.

Community Engagement

I have always had a stong inclination towards building community and collective action in my day-today personal life and past endeavors such as through volunteering, my position as The Community Resilience and Justice Specialist for Rio Grande Return, and invovlement in community engaged academic projects. As the Community Resilience and Justice Specialist I am responsible for engaging the public in the field of restoration through volunteer cordination and community outreach initatives such as organizing educational events, community science initatives, and expanding volunteer programs.
I also regularly volunteer with Tree New Mexico a non-profit based in Albuquerque that organizes tree plantings around the city and is currently working to expand their planting program statewide. I've volunteered with Tree NM since 2022 in various diffent capacities such as: planting team leader, data collector, flyering and canvassing,and facilitating educational events.

Interactive Web Map

Currently I have been developing an interactive web map by employing computer programming skills and Geographics Information Systems applications to create a map depicting food access in New Mexico. My groups map is try create a more holistic understanding of food acccess by integrating other food sources that typically aren't included in interactive web mapping methodologies related to food access including factors such as aggricultural lands and local farming operations, Community supported Aggriculture (CSA's), livestock herding, community gardens, etc.
